Electric City Hacks at Trent University 2018


This was my second time at Electric City Hacks, the other time being completely new to programming, thus not completing much. This year however, I planned to actually make something useful. Not much changed from last year in terms of awesomeness and the great organization by the school's students. Trent has an amazing campus, and our team took some time to get some excersise and fresh air near the river. The six hour drive was definetly worth the time as it was a great weekend where I learned many new skills, escpecially in web development.

For the first couple of hours, our team struggled to come up with any solid idea, bouncing around some crazy and dumb things until we decided to look at things taught in school. We remembered learning in our 11 AP physics self study unit about sound waves, and how inverse waves could cancel eachother out. We researched noise cancelling technology in headphones and in the end, decided on a unique idea of using that property to "encrypt" audio messages. Basically one user would send a recording to our server, which would apply a random, distorted sound to make the original audio indistiguishable. The end user, and only that user, would recieve and inverse sound wave, allowing them to cancel out the distortion and uncover the original message.
